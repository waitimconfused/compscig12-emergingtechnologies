Multimodal User Interfaces

Provides the user with multiple modes of interacting with a system, such as speech.

Accessibility

Opening up devices to be usable by all is extremely important in the age of computers and the internet.

Multimodal user interfaces aims to provide different means of interacting with computer systems. Some of which are:

  • Speech (natural communication)
  • Pen (like drawing tablets)
  • Touch (touchscreens)
  • Manual gestures (like ones gaze and head and body movements)

Things like speech and manual gestures are very key terms for accessibility.

Methods of input like manual gestures use machine learning algorithms to detect eye/body movements and translate them into typical inputs like mouse movements and keyboard interaction. These are extremely helpful to those who have physical disabilities.

Outputs, not just inputs

Multimodal User Interfaces can also give outputs, not just receive inputs like the ones listed above.

When interacting with an interface, feedback (visual/auditory) is vital. When you press a button, you expect something to happen, even if nothing obvious is triggered. The inclusion of visual or auditory feedback is still required by all, even with those who don't have disabilities.
