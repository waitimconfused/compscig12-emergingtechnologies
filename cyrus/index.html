Generative AI

Generative AI is the process of using artificial intelligence to generate something. Most commonly generative AI is used to generate images and videos, literature and even software. Generative AI doesn’t critically think about what it is doing, only picking the most likely output based on the input and the previous state. For example AI like ChatGPT uses your prompt to provide an answer and constructs a sentence based off of the sentence it had written so far by picking the most likely word to come after it. Generative AI has massive effects on the world economy, just last month it reached a valuation of over 850 billion dollars, that is more money than some countries have and that is just one company that is working on generative AI. Furthermore, we see impacts on Canadian education, with the university of Calgary trying to implement AI into their course and have their educators learn to use AI as a tool to lighten the load of teaching on them. However, generative AI isn’t without its problems, recently we have seen AI be weaponized against different minorities and cultures, just recently we had seen Iran use AI to spread misinformation about their military feats. Many accounts on X began showing videos of missiles striking Tel Aviv. However upon further inspection by journalists and experts we find that the videos are actually AI generated. This caused massive chaos on the internet with political discussions on if the US and Israel should continue with the attack on Iran. Additionally AI has also negative impacts on education, the company turnitin found with it’s AI detection tool, 10% of all assignments that were handed in had some level of generative AI and in 3% of all assignments were more than 80% made of AI generated content. This shows that some students aren’t engaging with the curriculum and cheating their way through our school system.

Quantum Computing

Quantum computing is computing using qubits instead of bits, allowing for a quantum state of 0, 1 or both simultaneously. This allows quantum computers to be much faster than traditional computers. This could greatly affect Canadian society because using shor’s algorithm, a quantum computer can crack certain encryption algorithms and lead to the world’s largest hacking events since all passwords would be compromised as soon as that happens.

Blockchain

The blockchain is a set of decentralized nodes, each one hashed with a certain check sum function. These blockchains are generally hard to alter since each block is “chained” to the previous one therefore if you wanted to change one it would affect every block afterwards as well as since it’s decentralized you would need to change the blocks of all other users who have the wrong node in order to fix the problem. This has a great impact on the Canadian economy since there are speculative alternative currencies based on the blockchain, fraud could easily ruin someone as the fraud would be nearly impossible to reverse.

Physical AI

Physical AI uses robots and other hardware in order to allow AI to interact with the physical world. Currently physical AI comes in many forms such as flippy which is able to work at fast food restaurants as their fry cook and at a better efficiency than actual people. This has a negative impact on developing countries since the physical labour jobs taken by native workers would be replaced by robots taking more money away from these developing countries.

Agentic AI

Agentic AI uses AI to achieve a goal given by the user autonomously without needing any further assistance from the user. Essentially agentic AI attempts to give AI the ability to critically think and learn from past experiences to improve. This greatly affects the world economy as agentic AI could replace a lot of jobs if given enough time and development. That would revolutionize the economy as we know it in a post job society.
