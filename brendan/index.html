Computer Vision

Computer vision is a subfield of AI that uses machine learning to analyze, process, and interpret visual data like images and videos. In the Canadian society, this technology is commonly used in security cameras, traffic management systems, as well as retail systems such as automated checkouts. While the systems have enhanced efficiency and safety, there have also been concerns over the issue of privacy due to the collection of information from the population.

Confidential Computing

Confidential computing is the protection of data using cloud technology to control encryption keys and isolate sensitive data in a protected CPU. This technology has the ability to affect the world economy by helping companies and financial institutions handle sensitive information securely through the cloud. This means that various companies across the world will be able to collaborate safely.

Bioinformatics

Bioinformatics involves the use of computers, software tools, and databases to generate large data sets that utilize genomics and proteomics.This technology can benefit minority groups in Canada, such as individuals with rare genetic conditions, by assisting scientists in studying their information to determine possible cures. Scientists can study large sets of biological information to better understand health conditions in small groups of society.

Extended Reality (XR)

Extended Reality covers all immersive technologies (VR, AR, MR) that allow people to engage with digital content through the combination of physical and digital worlds. In developing countries, XR technology can be used to increase access to education and professional training through the development of virtual classrooms. This will enable people to learn skills such as medicine, engineering, and technology despite the lack of such equipment.

Green Technology

Positives

The positive effects of green technology include economic growth. The World Economic Forum indicates that the world's green economy is now valued at over $5 trillion and is likely to reach over $7 trillion by the year 2030, with the revenues from the green economy growing at twice the rate of conventional businesses, hence helping companies attract investments, reduce capital costs, and also create jobs in the renewable energy sector.

Another positive effect of green technology is the conservation of natural resources. Green technology helps in conserving natural resources through the use of renewable energy, efficient energy, and effective waste management technologies. Green technology helps in the development of a circular economy, which enables the sustainability of the environment for future generations and helps in meeting the needs of the current population.

Negatives

The negative effect is that green technologies come with a high price tag. According to the report, it is estimated that 20% of key deep decarbonization technologies, such as low-carbon hydrogen and carbon capture, still have major cost disadvantages and will require significant support to become cost-competitive, thereby slowing their development, especially in developing countries, and limiting access to the full benefits of green technologies.

Despite the intentions of green technologies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, there is also the issue of the amount of waste generated when the lifespan of the technology is reached. The solar panels, the blades of the wind turbines, and the batteries of the electric vehicles contain harmful chemicals such as lead and cadmium, which pollute the soil and water when the technology is discarded and recycling facilities are limited.
