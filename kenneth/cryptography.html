Post-quantum Cryptography

Quantum computers will be able to decrypt encrypted information.
NIST is creating defenses against such attacks through Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC).

Quantum Computing vs. Current Cryptography

Who will win?

Our computers use a system of binary, where all data is represented with 0's and 1's.
With quantum computers (more specifically quantum physics), data is both a 0 and 1 at the same time.

This allows calculations that would be difficult (or impossible) on a conventional computer. Because of this, quantum computers could easily "crack" current cryptography algorithms.

Quantum Computing vs. Post-Quantum Encryption

To prevent quantum computers from wreaking havoc on our current cryptography algorithms, we need a better algorithm.

Better algorithms are being developed by NITS, who have released 3 PQC standards in 2024. They urge computer system administrators to begin transitioning to the new standards as soon as possible.

Impacts

Canadian Society (majority)

Implementing PQC may require new hiring, external contractors, or the realignment of roles that could affect other projects or work activities.

Because of the positives of implementing PQC, it is a net positive even if some people may lose their jobs through the realignment of roles.
